Having been born into a family with three generations of engineers, curiosity was the natural mood of the home I grew up in. I embarked on the journey of robotics in high school when I started building RC planes to satiate my passion for aviation.

Reading about electronics and flight dynamics, I started building fixed-wing RC planes from scratch. The process of fabricating air-worthy aircraft frames and integrating it with necessary electronics to watch them take flight built my passion-filled foundation for a career in robotics engineering.


To fortify my hobby-turned-passion, I pursued undergraduate studies in Mechanical Engineering where I learnt subjects like mechatronics, machine design, production process, structured programming approach and industrial electronics.


I signed up for a Python Programming course right after my undergraduate studeis which I can say with conviction was a pivot point in defining my career path as it not only gave me the tool to start automating simple robots through a raspberry pi microcontroller, but also dive into machine learning and computer vision.


Robotics, Python and Machine Learning bridge the gap between my hobby and passion, which is to build autonomous vehicles.
